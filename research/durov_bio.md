# Pavel Durov — Biography (Context Notes)

These notes are for translation consistency and quick reference (the book’s narrative covers mostly 2006–2012).

## Quick profile
- **Name**: Pavel Valeryevich Durov (Павел Валерьевич Дуров)
- **Born**: 1984-10-10, Leningrad (now St. Petersburg), USSR/Russia
- **Known for**: Founder of VKontakte (VK) and Telegram; outspoken on privacy / anti-censorship

## Family / early environment
- **Father**: Valery Durov (Валерий Дуров), philologist / academic
- **Brother**: Nikolai Durov (Николай Дуров), mathematician / programmer; later key technical contributor
- **Childhood setting** (per book’s Chapter 1 framing): St. Petersburg outskirts, strong reading/culture emphasis; “intellectual” family vibe contrasted with rough neighborhood dynamics.

## Education & early projects (pre-VK)
- Studied at **St. Petersburg State University (SPbSU)**.
- Ran / participated in student online communities and projects (e.g., **durov.com** communities mentioned early in the book).
- Built reputation for sharp rhetoric, provocation-as-style, and elite meritocratic self-image (theme recurring throughout the book).

## VK timeline (high level)
- **2006**: VKontakte launched (initially invitation-based, student focus).
- **2007–2011**: Rapid growth; monetization and platform/product debates; increasing attention from major Russian internet players and regulators.
- **2011–2012**: Heightened social/political pressure in Russia (protests era), with recurring public discussion of censorship vs. user freedom; VK becomes a major communications surface.

## Post-book context (for translator notes; outside main book span)
- **2013**: Telegram begins (mobile-first messaging; privacy/security positioning).
- **2014**: Durov leaves VK / Russia amid ownership and political pressure disputes.
- **2020s**: Telegram becomes a global-scale platform; Durov remains a high-profile, polarizing tech figure.

## Name transliteration conventions (recommended)
- **Durov**: Дуров → **Durov** (EN), **杜罗夫** (ZH), **ドゥーロフ** (JA)
- **Pavel**: Павел → **Pavel** (EN), **帕维尔** (ZH), **パーヴェル** (JA)
