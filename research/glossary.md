## Tone/Register Markers

### Durov's Speaking Style
- **Direct**: Use straightforward language
- **Technical**: Precise when discussing technology
- **Provocative**: Can be confrontational
- **Intellectual**: References philosophy, literature

### Narrative Voice
- **Journalistic**: Observational, detailed
- **Literary**: Poetic descriptions (especially Chapter 1)
- **Analytical**: Business/strategy sections

## Translation Principles

### Consistency Rules
1. **Names**: Use same transliteration throughout
2. **VKontakte**: Never translate, always "VKontakte" or "VK"
3. **Technical Terms**: Use standard tech terminology
4. **Cultural Terms**: Add explanatory notes when needed
5. **Idioms**: Localize rather than literal translation

### Special Considerations

**For English**:
- American English spelling
- Tech industry register
- Explain Russian cultural references

**For Chinese (Simplified)**:
- Mainland internet culture references
- 程序员 (programmer) culture
- Explain Western tech scene when needed

**For Japanese**:
- Balance formal/informal matching original
- Katakana for foreign names/terms
- Explain Russian cultural context

## Context Notes

### Don Quixote Reference
- Appears in Chapter 1 opening
- Symbolizes Durov as idealist fighting windmills
- Father's philology background connection
- Important thematic element

### Gopnik Culture
- Working-class youth subculture
- Associated with: tracksuit, squatting, aggression
- Class marker in Soviet/post-Soviet society
- Fear/avoidance in Chapter 1

### Panel Housing
- Soviet-era prefabricated apartments
- Visual: uniform concrete blocks, 5-16 stories
- Social marker: working/middle class
- Context for Durov's childhood

### Singer Building
- Historic St. Petersburg landmark
- Art Nouveau style, globe on top
- VK office on 6th floor
- Symbolic: old commerce → new digital economy

## Avoided Translations

**Do NOT translate these**:
- VKontakte / VK (brand name)
- Yandex, Mail.ru (brand names)
- Nevsky Prospekt (proper noun)
- Singer Building (proper noun)
- "Durov.com" (website name)

## Localization Strategy

### When to Explain vs. Translate
- **Explain**: Cultural references unique to Russia
- **Translate**: Universal concepts with local terms
- **Keep Original**: Brand names, proper nouns
- **Add Notes**: Historical/political context

### Target Reader Assumptions
- **English**: Tech-savvy, knows Facebook/Silicon Valley
- **Chinese**: Familiar with WeChat/QQ, Chinese internet
- **Japanese**: Knows Mixi/LINE, Japanese tech culture
- All readers: Need Russian cultural context explained
