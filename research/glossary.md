## Translation Principles

### Proper Names
- **Keep Russian names in transliteration** (Latin alphabet)
- **Add local translations** in parentheses on first mention
- **Use standard transliterations** for well-known figures/places

### Technical Terms
- **Use established translations** where they exist
- **Maintain consistency** across all four languages
- **Prefer clarity over literal translation**

### Cultural References
- **Translator notes** for unfamiliar concepts
- **Brief explanations** for cultural context
- **Avoid over-explaining** - trust reader intelligence

### Tone & Style
- **Preserve Durov's voice**: Sharp, direct, unconventional
- **Maintain narrative flow**: Don't let explanations interrupt
- **Adapt idioms**: Don't translate literally
- **Keep humor**: Find equivalent expressions

## Notes on Usage

### Chinese Considerations
- Use Simplified Chinese (mainland standard)
- Consider 程序员 (programmer) culture
- Use appropriate internet slang (网络用语)
- Balance formal and informal registers

### Japanese Considerations
- Use appropriate level of politeness (です・ます form generally)
- Katakana for foreign names and some tech terms
- Consider Japanese tech culture parallels
- Use appropriate pronouns and honorifics

### English Considerations
- American English spelling (Stanford reader context)
- Tech industry idioms where appropriate
- Avoid British-isms
- Maintain informal but intelligent tone
