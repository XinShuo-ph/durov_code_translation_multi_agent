# Chapter Summaries

## Preface (Предисловие)
By Yuri Saprykin. Introduces the book as an adventure novel about a "hero of our time" – Pavel Durov. Describes Durov as an enigmatic figure, and VKontakte as a phenomenon that defines the reality for millions of young Russians.

## Prologue (Пролог)
A scene in the Zinger House (VK HQ). Durov and his team are negotiating with an "expat" manager from a large internet holding (likely Mail.ru). Durov plays the role of a "bad cop" or eccentric, demanding higher percentages for a deal, then abruptly threatening to leave. Highlights his unconventional negotiation style, the unique "sect-like" atmosphere of the VK team, and Durov's disdain for traditional corporate rules.

## Chapter 1: Botanical Garden (Ботанический сад)
Focuses on Durov's childhood.
- Describes the bleak St. Petersburg outskirts where he grew up.
- Introduces his family: father Valery (philologist), brother Nikolai (math genius).
- Time in Turin, Italy: Father got a job there. Pavel attended school in Italy, experienced a different, freer culture.
- Return to Russia: Contrast between Italy and post-Soviet Russia.
- School years: Pavel's rebellious nature (changing computer wallpapers to insult teachers), high intelligence, feeling of being an outsider ("aristocrat" among "gopniks").

## Chapter 2: The Architect (Архитектор)
University years at St. Petersburg State University (Philological Faculty).
- Military training (ROTC): Pavel studied propaganda and psychological warfare. Learned how to manipulate information.
- Early web projects:
    - **durov.com**: Library of exam answers (helping students cheat/prepare).
    - **spbgu.ru**: University forum. Pavel used sockpuppets (fake accounts) to incite arguments and drive engagement. He realized the power of user-generated content and social dynamics.
- Friendships: Vyacheslav Mirilashvili (Slava) and Lev Leviev (fellow students).

## Chapter 3: In Contact (В Контакте)
The founding of VKontakte (2006).
- Slava Mirilashvili returns from the US, sees the success of Facebook.
- Slava contacts Pavel (having seen his forum success).
- They decide to build a Russian social network. Pavel provides the code/vision, Slava and Lev provide the capital (from Slava's father's business).
- Launch: October 10, 2006. Closed registration (invite only) initially to create exclusivity.
- Rapid growth: University by university. The name "VKontakte" chosen to be neutral.

## Chapter 4: The Wall (Стена)
Growth and scaling (2007-2010).
- Technical challenges: Keeping up with millions of users. Nikolai Durov's role in optimizing code (KPHP).
- Features: The "Wall", photo tagging, audio/video (which led to piracy issues but massive growth).
- Monetization dilemma: Pavel resisted ads, wanted a clean interface. Investors pushed for revenue.
- Team culture: Meritocracy, small team of elite coders, working late nights in the Zinger House.

## Chapter 5: War (Война)
The conflict with Mail.ru Group.
- Yuri Milner and Mail.ru buy stakes from Slava and Lev.
- Mail.ru wants full control (100% acquisition).
- Durov resists integration. Famous photo of him giving the finger to Mail.ru.
- Durov manages to convince shareholders to give him voting rights over their shares, solidifying his control despite owning a minority stake.

## Chapter 6: Churov and Durov (Чуров и Дуров)
Political pressure (2011-2012).
- Parliamentary elections, allegations of fraud (Churov was head of Election Commission).
- Mass protests organized via VK.
- FSB demands Durov shut down opposition groups (e.g., Alexei Navalny's).
- Durov refuses publicly, posting a picture of a dog in a hoodie with its tongue out.
- The concept of "information neutrality" – refusing to censor.

## Chapter 7: The Other (Другой)
Future outlook (as of ~2012).
- Durov's worldview: Libertarianism, removing boundaries.
- The "Donate" experiment (throwing money from the window – a controversial incident).
- Hints at global ambitions (Telegram precursors).
- Conclusion: Durov as a "totem", a symbol of the new digital generation that cannot be controlled by old methods.
