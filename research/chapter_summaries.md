# Chapter Summaries: The Durov Code

## Preface (Предисловие)
By Yuri Saprykin. Introduces the book as an adventure novel about a "nerd" revolution. Discusses how VKontakte became a "central nervous system" for Russian youth and how Durov represents a new type of leader—one who dictates terms to the old world.

## Prologue (Пролог)
Opens with a dramatic scene in the Singer House (VK HQ). Durov and his team are negotiating with a major e-commerce player. Durov plays "bad cop", shouts "20 percent!", and walks out, showing his unconventional and aggressive negotiation style. Introduces the core team dynamic and the atmosphere of the "botanical garden" (the office of nerds).

## Chapter 1: Botanical Garden (Глава 1)
Covers Durov's childhood.
- Born in Leningrad, spent years in Turin, Italy.
- Brother Nikolai is introduced as a math prodigy.
- Return to Russia, enrolling in the Academic Gymnasium (experimental school).
- Early traits: Rebellious against authority (teachers), prankster (screensavers), competitive.
- Interest in coding and design.

## Chapter 2: The Totem (Глава 2)
University years at St. Petersburg State University (SPbGU), Philology Department.
- Enrolled in military faculty (specializing in psychological warfare/propaganda).
- Created `durov.com` (library for students) and `spbgu.ru` (university forum).
- **Key Strategy**: Used fake accounts to stimulate debates on the forum, learning how to manage online communities.
- Won the Potanin scholarship but despised the official ceremonies.

## Chapter 3: The Wall (Глава 3)
The founding of VKontakte.
- Reconnected with old schoolmate Vyacheslav Mirilashvili (Slava), who saw Facebook in the US.
- Lev Leviev joins as co-founder/investor.
- Decision to build a Russian social network. Nikolai helps with the backend.
- **Launch**: October 2006. Originally closed registration, then opened.
- "VKontakte" name chosen to be inclusive.
- Explosive growth, overtaking competitors.
- First major investment from Yuri Milner (DST/Mail.ru).

## Chapter 4: The Fortress (Глава 4)
Scaling the startup.
- Moving into the Singer House.
- Technical challenges: Nikolai's custom C++ engines to handle massive load (KPHP).
- Team culture: Small, highly efficient, "spartan" but well-paid.
- Product philosophy: Speed and simplicity.
- The "Wall" redesign: Durov removes the user rating bar, causing user outrage ("Durov, return the wall!"), but sticking to his vision.

## Chapter 5: War (Глава 5)
Conflict with investors and the state.
- Mail.ru Group (Usmanov/Milner) wants to acquire 100% of VK and merge it with Odnoklassniki.
- Durov resists integration.
- **The Gesture**: Posts a photo of himself giving the finger to Mail.ru.
- **2011 Protests**: FSB demands VK block opposition groups (Navalny). Durov refuses, tweeting a dog in a hoodie.
- Police come to his apartment; he refuses to open the door.

## Chapter 6: The Code (Глава 6)
Durov's worldview and lifestyle.
- Libertarian views: "The government is a service provider."
- **Money incident**: Throwing paper planes made of 5000-ruble notes from the Singer House window, causing a crowd fight.
- **The "Hit and Run"**: Accusation of running over a policeman. Durov claims fabrication.
- Entry of UCP (United Capital Partners) as a hostile shareholder (buying out Slava and Lev).
- Durov begins working on a "Plan B" (Telegram) using a separate legal entity (Digital Fortress).

## Chapter 7: Another Sky (Глава 7)
The endgame and future.
- Growing pressure from UCP and the state.
- Durov's vision of a supra-national digital state.
- Launch of Telegram: Focus on encryption and independence.
- The book ends (published 2012/2013) with Durov still at VK but the storm clouds gathering, hinting at his eventual departure and the global potential of his ideas.

## About the Author
Nikolai Kononov is a journalist who spent months interviewing Durov and his team, getting unprecedented access to the secretive company.
