## Предисловие / Preface (Pages 5-6)

**Author**: Implied to be Yuri Saprykin (editor at Afisha)

**Summary**: The preface laments that Russia's dramatic post-Soviet stories go undocumented. Describes VKontakte as an invisible world that dominates young people's lives. Celebrates the book as a rare documentation of a contemporary Russian success story.

**Key themes**:
- Russia as "country of lost narratives"
- VK's ubiquity among youth (under-20 demographic)
- Durov as embodiment of digital elite
- Historical documentation value

**Key quotes**:
- "Нынешняя Россия – это страна потерянных сюжетов" (Today's Russia is a country of lost narratives)
- "цифровая элита" (digital elite)

**Tone**: Intellectual, slightly ironic, journalistic

## Пролог / Prologue (Pages 7-12)

**Setting**: VKontakte headquarters, Singer House, 6th floor, St. Petersburg

**Summary**: A tense negotiation scene between VK executives and a foreign e-commerce company executive. The foreign manager wants to sell products through VK's "Желания" (Wishes) gift service but offers unfavorable terms. Pavel Durov, disguised as a quiet figure in a baseball cap, suddenly explodes demanding 20% commission or no deal, shocking the composed foreign businessman.

**Key characters introduced**:
- The negotiator at the window (described as "attack dog")
- The programmer with laptop and iPhone (main developer)
- The quiet guy in baseball cap (Durov)
- Foreign e-commerce executive (unnamed)

**Key events**:
- Three-hour negotiation
- Discussion of "Желания" (Wishes) service
- Durov's explosive demand for 20%

**Narrative technique**: Opens in medias res, creates mystery about who's in charge

**Key terms**:
- "attack dog" - hardball negotiator
- "экспат" - expat manager

## Глава 1: Ботанический сад / Chapter 1: The Botanical Garden (Pages 13-22)

**Summary**: Pavel Durov's childhood in the remote outskirts of St. Petersburg. Describes a lonely, bookish boy navigating a harsh urban landscape of unfinished construction, swamps, and threatening "gopniks." Shows his intellectual isolation, love of Cervantes, and the influence of his mathematician brother Nikolai.

**Key themes**:
- Isolation and intellectualism
- Hostile environment (физическая и социальная)
- Brother Nikolai as prodigy (reading astronomy at age 3)
- St. Petersburg's rough suburban landscape
- Survival strategies for a "botanik" among gopniks

**Key locations**:
- New housing district near Baltic Sea
- Abandoned military airfield
- Underground metro construction site
- Bunkers and bomb shelters

**Literary devices**:
- Chapter title is double meaning: "Botanical Garden" = nerd territory
- "Ботаник" (botanist/nerd) living in literal wild space

**Key imagery**:
- Boy with Cervantes book on bunker roof
- Gopniks by the bonfires
- Teens building rafts across flooded streets

## Глава 2: Архитектор / Chapter 2: The Architect (Pages 23-37)

**Summary**: Durov's university years at St. Petersburg State University's Faculty of Philology. Details his early entrepreneurial ventures including the "Olymp" beauty contest (co-organized with Ilya Perekopsky), the mysterious "Durov.com Club" with black business cards, and the student forum that preceded VKontakte.

**Key themes**:
- Early networking and self-promotion
- Creating mystique and exclusivity
- University as launching pad
- Unconventional approaches to organizing

**Key events**:
- "Olymp" beauty contest for students
- Distribution of enigmatic black business cards
- Student forum development

**Key characters**:
- Ilya Perekopsky (future VK VP)
- Female students lining up for contest

**Title meaning**: "Architect" = Durov designing social structures

---

## Глава 3: Мафия открывает глаза / Chapter 3: The Mafia Opens Its Eyes (Pages 38-50)

**Summary**: The story of VKontakte's first investors. Vyacheslav (Slava) Mirilashvili, heir to a St. Petersburg business family with a complicated past (father's kidnapping, prison), discovers Durov through a newspaper interview. Together with Lev Leviev, they invest in the nascent social network.

**Key themes**:
- Russian business dynasties
- Privatization-era wealth
- Family trauma and resilience
- Smart money finding opportunities

**Key characters**:
- Slava Mirilashvili (investor, Tufts graduate)
- Lev Leviev (partner, McGill graduate, Ernst & Young)
- Mikhail Mirilashvili (Slava's father)
- Elnara Petrova (journalist who interviewed Durov)

**Key events**:
- Mirilashvili reading about Durov in "Delovoy Peterburg"
- Payment terminal business (abandoned)
- Decision to invest in VKontakte

**Title meaning**: "Mafia" here = social deduction game reference + investor network awakening

## Глава 4: Сок черешни / Chapter 4: Cherry Juice (Pages 51-64)

**Summary**: The intense early days of VKontakte development. Describes the cramped apartment-office on Nevsky Prospekt where programmers coded through the night. Shows the company's growth from small team to expanding operation, culminating in move to Singer House.

**Key themes**:
- Startup intensity
- Coding culture and all-nighters
- Team dynamics
- Rapid growth challenges

**Key settings**:
- Apartment office (150 sq meters, 4 rooms)
- 9 sq meter coding room
- Loft near Fontanka and Nevsky

**Key events**:
- Milner's DST investment (fall 2007)
- Property purchases with mother's help
- Move to Singer House

**Key characters**:
- Nikolai Durov (coding in armchair with chocolate bars)
- Core programming team
- Lev visiting with food deliveries

**Title meaning**: "Cherry Juice" - possibly reference to late-night snacks/energy

## Глава 5: Стена / Chapter 5: The Wall (Pages 65-78)

**Summary**: The corporate world closes in. Introduces Dmitry Grishin and Mail.ru's history, from his early game development as a student to becoming CEO. Details the complex ownership structure and growing tensions between VKontakte's founders and corporate shareholders.

**Key themes**:
- Corporate vs. startup culture
- Russian internet consolidation
- Power struggles
- Grishin's origin story (parallel to Durov)

**Key characters**:
- Dmitry Grishin (Mail.ru CEO)
- Yuri Milner (DST founder)
- Port.ru and NetBridge merger history

**Key events**:
- Grishin's first game sale in Saratov (9th grade)
- 2001 dotcom crash in Russia
- Mail.ru Group formation

**Title meaning**: "The Wall" = barrier between startup idealism and corporate reality

## Глава 6: Запрещенный прием / Chapter 6: Forbidden Move (Pages 79-91)

**Summary**: Philosophy, violence, and confrontation. Features Ravdonikas's essay about potential violence on the streets, juxtaposed with a real street fight involving Durov, Ravdonikas, and Dobromyslov after February 23rd celebrations. Shows the mix of intellectual posturing and real danger in Durov's world.

**Key themes**:
- Violence and intellectualism
- St. Petersburg street culture
- Bohemian tech scene
- Masculinity and confrontation

**Key events**:
- Trumpeter on Nevsky driving office crazy
- February 23, 2011 street altercation
- "Got a cigarette?" confrontation

**Key characters**:
- Ravdonikas (essayist)
- Dobromyslov (designer)
- Gopniks outside Subway

**Key literary elements**:
- Ravdonikas's meta-essay about violence
- Blurring of fiction and reality

**Title meaning**: "Forbidden Move" = breaking rules of engagement

## Глава 7: Пятно Роршаха / Chapter 7: Rorschach Blot (Pages 92-98)

**Summary**: Looking to the future. Durov's failed attempt to expand VK globally (Studi VZ in Germany), realization that competing with Facebook internationally is futile, and early thinking about mobile messaging (precursor to Telegram). Introduces his philosophy of "tweaking" rather than inventing.

**Key themes**:
- Global ambitions and limitations
- Russian identity in tech
- Innovation philosophy
- Future directions

**Key events**:
- Purchase of vk.com domain
- Failed Studi VZ acquisition
- Mobile messaging concept development

**Key references**:
- Malcolm Gladwell on Steve Jobs as "tweaker"
- WhatsApp and iMessage as competitors
- "Subvert The Paradigm" motto

**Title meaning**: "Rorschach Blot" = Durov as projection of viewer's interpretation

## Narrative Arc Summary

1. **Hook**: VK's power demonstrated through negotiation scene
2. **Origins**: Isolated intellectual child survives hostile environment
3. **Education**: University as platform for early experiments
4. **Investment**: Meeting money (Mirilashvili connects)
5. **Growth**: Intense startup phase, rapid scaling
6. **Conflict**: Corporate pressures mount
7. **Crisis**: Street violence echoes business battles
8. **Resolution**: Looking beyond Russia, toward future
