# Chapter Summaries

## Chapter 1: Botanical Garden (Ботанический сад)
**Pages 13-22**
Focuses on Pavel Durov's childhood and early formation. Opens with a scene of a boy reading Cervantes in the grim outskirts of St. Petersburg (Primorsky district). Covers his family's time in Turin, Italy, where Pavel experienced a different educational system. After returning to Russia, he attends the Academic Gymnasium. Introduces his brother Nikolai, a math prodigy, and their intellectual environment.

## Chapter 2: The Architect (Архитектор)
**Pages 23-37**
Covers Durov's years at St. Petersburg State University (SPbGU), Philology Department. He launches `durov.com` (a database of exam answers) and `spbgu.ru` (a university forum). On the forum, he orchestrates debates using sockpuppet accounts to increase engagement, showing his early grasp of community dynamics. Vyacheslav Mirilashvili returns from the US, sees the potential of social networks (Facebook), and reconnects with Durov.

## Chapter 3: Mafia Opens Its Eyes (Мафия открывает глаза)
**Pages 38-50**
The founding of VKontakte. Mirilashvili, Lev Leviev, and Durov form the company. They rent an apartment on Nevsky Prospect to use as an office. Describes the frantic coding sessions, the launch in October 2006, and the initial invite-only strategy. The title references the game "Mafia", popular among the team.

## Chapter 4: Cherry Juice (Сок черешни)
**Pages 51-64**
Describes the explosive growth of VKontakte and the technical challenges of scaling. The team expands but maintains a hacker culture (sleeping in the office, night work). Describes the unique atmosphere of the early VK team. "Cherry Juice" might refer to a specific memory or detail from their office life (possibly a favorite drink or a code word).

## Chapter 5: The Wall (Стена)
**Pages 65-78**
Focuses on product evolution and conflicts. The removal of the classic "Wall" feature and its replacement with "Microblog" causes a massive user revolt ("Durov, return the wall!"). This chapter also details the growing tension with investors (Mail.ru Group) who want more monetization and control. Durov resists, prioritizing user experience over profits.

## Chapter 6: Forbidden Move (Запрещенный прием)
**Pages 79-91**
The political turning point. Covers the 2011-2012 protests in Russia. The FSB demands that Durov block opposition groups on VK. Durov refuses, famously posting a photo of a dog in a hoodie with its tongue out. The chapter depicts the increasing pressure from the state and the "war" for control of the company.

## Chapter 7: Rorschach Spot (Пятно Роршаха)
**Pages 92-98**
Durov's realization that VK has limited global potential due to its Russian roots. Failed attempts to acquire German competitor StudiVZ. Durov begins to look beyond Russia. Introduction of the "Zinc" project (a secure communication tool), which would eventually become Telegram. The book ends with Durov still at VK but with his eyes set on the future.

## About Author (Об авторе)
**Page 99**
Brief biography of Nikolai Kononov, the journalist who wrote the book.
