# Russian Cultural and Historical Context

## Overview
This document provides context for understanding the cultural, political, and social references in "Код Дурова" (Durov Code), essential for accurate translation.

---

## Historical Period Covered (1984-2012)

### Late Soviet Era (1984-1991)
- **Perestroika**: Gorbachev's reforms began 1985
- **Glasnost**: Increased openness and transparency
- **1991**: USSR dissolution; Russian Federation formed
- Birth year of Pavel Durov (1984) falls in this transition

### 1990s ("The Wild Nineties")
- **Economic collapse**: Hyperinflation, poverty
- **Privatization**: State assets transferred to oligarchs
- **Бандитизм (Banditry)**: Organized crime flourished
- **"Крыша" (Roof)**: Protection rackets common
- Many references in book to this chaotic period

### 2000s Putin Era
- **1999-2000**: Putin rises to power
- **Stability**: Economic growth from oil revenues
- **Consumer boom**: Referenced in book (Chapter 3)
- **Internet growth**: RuNet (Russian internet) develops
- **"Силовики" (Siloviki)**: Security service officials gain power

## St. Petersburg Context

### City Significance
- **Former name**: Leningrad (until 1991)
- **Status**: Russia's "cultural capital"
- **Putin's hometown**: Many connections to power
- **Tech hub**: Developing IT sector in 2000s

### Locations in the Book

#### Singer House (Дом Зингера)
- **Address**: Nevsky Prospekt 28
- Historic building, former Singer sewing machine HQ
- Now houses VKontakte offices (6th floor) and Dom Knigi bookstore
- Iconic Art Nouveau architecture with glass globe on top
- Key symbol in the book

#### Nevsky Prospekt
- Main avenue of St. Petersburg
- Center of city life, commerce, culture
- Durov's world revolves around this area

#### Housing Districts (Новостройки)
- Durov grew up in new housing developments on outskirts
- Панельные дома (panel apartment buildings)
- Soviet-era mass housing, often isolated
- References to marshland, Baltic Sea proximity
- "Камышовая", "Ситцевая" - street names mentioned

#### St. Petersburg State University (СПбГУ)
- One of Russia's oldest and most prestigious universities
- Филологический факультет (Faculty of Philology) - Durov's faculty
- Traditional rival to Moscow State University

## Russian Internet Culture (RuNet)

### Key Terms
- **RuNet (Рунет)**: Russian-language internet
- **Юзер**: User (from English)
- **Контент**: Content
- **Аккаунт**: Account
- **Залить**: To upload

### Major Platforms Before VK
- **Rambler**: Portal and search engine
- **Yandex**: Search engine (now tech giant)
- **Mail.ru**: Email and portal
- **LiveJournal (Живой Журнал/ЖЖ)**: Blogging platform, hugely popular
- **Odnoklassniki**: Social network for classmates (est. 2006)

### Youth Internet Slang
- **Школота**: Derogatory term for young/immature users
- **Падонки**: Counter-culture movement with intentional misspellings
- **Лол (LOL)**: Laughing
- **Кек**: Expression of laughter (later)
- **ЛС (Личное сообщение)**: Private message

## Social Classes and Types

### Гопники (Gopniks)
- **Definition**: Working-class youth subculture
- **Appearance**: Tracksuits (Adidas), squatting pose
- **Behavior**: Aggressive, territorial, confrontational
- **Threat**: Real danger for "nerdy" kids like young Durov
- **Common phrase**: "Есть закурить?" (Got a cigarette?) - opening for confrontation

### Ботаник (Botanik/Nerd)
- **Literal meaning**: "Botanist"
- **Slang meaning**: Nerd, bookish person
- Chapter 1 title "Ботанический сад" (Botanical Garden) plays on this

### Интеллигенция (Intelligentsia)
- Educated, cultured class
- Durov family belongs to this (father is professor)
- Traditional Russian respect for education and culture

### Новые русские (New Russians)
- Nouveau riche of 1990s
- Often connected to privatization or crime
- Flashy wealth display

## Business Culture References

### "Крыша" (Roof/Protection)
- Protection arrangement with criminals or authorities
- Mentioned in Chapter 3 context
- Shifted from бандиты to силовики in 2000s

### Олигархи (Oligarchs)
- Ultra-wealthy businessmen from 1990s privatization
- Usmanov (Mail.ru) belongs to this class
- Complex relationship with state power

### Потанинская стипендия (Potanin Scholarship)
- Prestigious scholarship from Potanin Foundation
- Durov won it three times consecutively
- Demonstrates exceptional academic achievement

## Historical References in Book

### Mirilashvili Family (Chapter 3)
- **Mikhail Mirilashvili**: St. Petersburg businessman
- Kidnapping incident in 2001
- "Kidnapping of kidnapper's father" - complex case
- Son Slava sent abroad for safety
- Family business in real estate and casinos

### February 23 (Chapter 6)
- **Defender of the Fatherland Day**
- Former Soviet Army Day
- Public holiday in Russia
- Often involves drinking, celebrations

### Russian Philosophy References
- Don Quixote (Сервантес/Cervantes) - Durov's favorite
- Libertarian influences
- Anti-authoritarian themes

## Corporate Entities

### Mail.ru Group
- Major Russian internet company
- Founded by Yuri Milner (DST)
- Eventually acquired VKontakte
- Now owns: VK, Odnoklassniki, Mail.ru email, games division

### Digital Sky Technologies (DST)
- Investment fund founded by Yuri Milner
- Early Facebook investor
- Invested in VKontakte 2007
- Later became DST Global
