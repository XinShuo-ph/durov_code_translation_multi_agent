# Russian Tech & Cultural Context

## Historical Background

### Post-Soviet Tech Landscape (1991-2000)
- Collapse of Soviet Union (1991) opened markets
- Brain drain of scientists and engineers
- Early internet adoption limited to academics
- Dial-up era, expensive access

### RuNet Emergence (2000-2010)
- "RuNet" (Рунет): Russian-language internet
- Yandex founded 1997, dominated search
- Mail.ru became email/portal leader
- Odnoklassniki (2006) first major social network
- VKontakte (2006) overtook competitors

## St. Petersburg Tech Scene

### Historical Significance
- "Northern Capital" - cultural/intellectual center
- Many universities (SPbSU, ITMO, Polytechnic)
- Strong math/physics tradition
- Alternative to Moscow's political focus

### Notable Tech Institutions
1. **St. Petersburg State University (SPbSU)**
   - Durov's alma mater
   - Strong CS and math programs
   - ACM ICPC success

2. **ITMO University**
   - Programming competition powerhouse
   - Multiple ACM ICPC world champions

### Singer Building (Дом Зингера)
- VK headquarters location
- Historic 1902-1904 Art Nouveau building
- On Nevsky Prospekt (main avenue)
- Symbolic of old-meets-new

## Political Context (2006-2014)

### Key Events Timeline
| Year | Event | Relevance to VK |
|------|-------|-----------------|
| 2008 | Medvedev becomes President | Tech-friendly rhetoric |
| 2011 | Duma elections, protests | VK groups organized opposition |
| 2012 | Putin returns as President | Increased pressure on media |
| 2013 | Snowden revelations | Privacy concerns global |
| 2014 | Ukraine crisis begins | VK data requests from FSB |
| 2014 | Durov leaves Russia | Direct result of pressure |

### Government Relationship with Tech
1. **Surveillance**: SORM system for monitoring
2. **Censorship**: Roskomnadzor blacklists
3. **Nationalism**: Pressure to store data in Russia
4. **Control**: Ownership shifts to state-friendly entities

## Generational Differences

### Soviet Generation (born pre-1970)
- Grew up under communism
- Distrust of institutions
- Survival mentality

### Transition Generation (1970-1985)
- Experienced collapse
- Adapted to market economy
- Many in current leadership

### Digital Natives (born post-1985)
- Durov's generation
- First to grow up with internet
- Different relationship with authority
- VK's core early users
