# Russian Cultural and Political Context

## Overview
This document provides context for understanding the cultural, political, and technological environment in which Pavel Durov grew up and VKontakte was created.

---

## Historical Timeline

### Soviet Era End (1991)
- USSR dissolved December 1991
- Pavel Durov was 7 years old
- Economic chaos, "shock therapy"
- Rapid privatization

### 1990s: Turbulent Transition
- Hyperinflation, economic hardship
- Rise of oligarchs
- Weak state institutions
- Cultural upheaval
- Durov family in Italy during part of this period

### 2000s: Putin Era Begins
- Vladimir Putin became president (2000)
- Economic stabilization (oil prices rising)
- "Managed democracy" emerging
- VKontakte founded in this period (2006)

### 2010s: Tightening Control
- 2011-2012 protests
- Increased internet regulation
- VK became politically significant
- Durov's conflicts with authorities

## St. Petersburg Context

### The City
- **Former Names**: Leningrad (Soviet), Petrograd (WWI), St. Petersburg (original/current)
- Russia's "cultural capital"
- European-influenced architecture and culture
- Historically liberal compared to Moscow
- Strong academic tradition

### Key Districts Mentioned in Book

#### Kupchino (Купчино)
- Southern suburban district
- Soviet-era mass housing
- Where Durov grew up (at least partly)
- "Спальный район" (bedroom community)
- Panel buildings (панельные дома)

#### Academic/University Areas
- St. Petersburg State University (main campus)
- Vasilievsky Island (university buildings)
- Intellectual atmosphere

### St. Petersburg Culture
- "Питер" (Piter) - colloquial name
- Known for: culture, literature, museums
- White nights (Белые ночи)
- Distinct from Moscow's commercial focus
- Tech scene developed here (VK, JetBrains)

## Russian Internet (RuNet)

### Early History
- 1990s: Internet arrives in Russia
- Dial-up era, limited access
- 2000s: Broadband expansion
- 2010s: Mobile internet boom

### Key Russian Sites (2000s-2010s)
| Site | Type | Notes |
|------|------|-------|
| Yandex | Search/portal | Russian Google |
| Mail.ru | Email/portal | Later acquired VK stake |
| Odnoklassniki | Social | "Classmates" - older users |
| VKontakte | Social | Youth focus |
| LiveJournal | Blogging | Politically influential |
| Habrahabr | Tech news | IT community |

### RuNet Culture
- Distinct meme culture
- Cyrillic internet slang
- Strong piracy culture
- Government monitoring concerns
- "Onion layers" of public/private communication

### Key Terms (Интернет-сленг)
- **лулз** (lulz): Laughs, entertainment value
- **тред** (thread): Discussion thread
- **паблик** (public): Public VK page
- **репост**: Repost/share
- **лайк**: Like
- **хейтер** (hater): Critic

## Russian Youth Culture (2000s)

### Education System
- 11-year school system
- Strong math/science emphasis
- University extremely competitive
- Academic gymnasiums (like Durov attended)

### Subcultures
| Term | Meaning | Context |
|------|---------|---------|
| **гопник** | Hooligan/chav | Street youth, often violent |
| **ботаник** | Nerd/geek | Studious person (can be negative) |
| **неформал** | Informal | Alternative subcultures |
| **мажор** | Rich kid | Wealthy, privileged youth |

### Technology Access
- 2000s: Computers becoming common
- Gaming cafes (компьютерные клубы)
- Pirated software widespread
- Programming self-taught common

## Business Culture

### Oligarch System
- Small group controls major assets
- Often political connections
- Complex ownership structures
- Not purely market-driven

### Tech Business Specifics
- Government interest in tech control
- Investment from oligarch-connected funds
- Less venture capital than Silicon Valley
- Often need government relationships

### Key Business Terms
- **рейдерство** (raiding): Hostile corporate takeovers
- **крыша** (roof): Protection, connections
- **откат** (kickback): Bribery
- **понятия** (understandings): Informal rules

---

## Political Context

### Government and Internet
- Initially hands-off approach to internet
- 2011-2012: Protests organized online
- After 2012: Increased regulation
- "Sovereign internet" concepts emerging

### Key Events
| Year | Event | Impact |
|------|-------|--------|
| 2011 | Duma election protests | VK used for organization |
| 2012 | Putin returns as president | Tightening begins |
| 2012 | "Foreign agents" law | NGO restrictions |
| 2014 | Crimea annexation | Geopolitical shift |
| 2014 | Durov leaves Russia | Symbolic moment |

### Censorship Context
- Soviet legacy of state control
- Post-Soviet media freedom (1990s)
- Gradual tightening (2000s-2010s)
- Roskomnadzor (internet regulator) established 2008

## Translation Challenges

### Untranslatable Concepts
- **тоска**: Deep melancholy (no English equivalent)
- **авось**: "Maybe it'll work out" (Russian fatalism)
- **интеллигенция**: Intellectual class (broader than English "intellectuals")
- **бытовуха**: Everyday mundane reality

## Key Figures in Russian Tech

### Yuri Milner
- Born 1961, Moscow
- Physicist turned entrepreneur
- Founded DST (Digital Sky Technologies)
- Early Facebook, Alibaba investor
- Key figure in VK investment

### Alisher Usmanov
- Born 1953, Uzbekistan
- Metals/mining oligarch
- Major Mail.ru shareholder
- Represented corporate interests in VK

### Arkady Volozh
- Yandex co-founder
- Russian tech pioneer
- Different path from Durov (more corporate)

- Durov represents libertarian/rebel strain unusual in Russia
- VK's success despite (not because of) environment
- Book captures pre-2014 Russia, different from current era
