# Russian Cultural and Political Context

## Historical Context (Book Period: 2006-2012)

### Putin Era
- Vladimir Putin: President 2000-2008, Prime Minister 2008-2012, President 2012-present
- Medvedev presidency (2008-2012): "modernization" rhetoric, tech-friendly
- Growing state control over media
- Oil-fueled economic growth
- Increasing internet penetration

### Economic Context
- Post-2008 financial crisis recovery
- Rising middle class
- Tech startup ecosystem emerging
- Venture capital entering Russia
- Mail.ru, Yandex as major internet companies

## Russian Internet (Runet) Landscape

### Major Players (2006-2012)
1. **Yandex** - Search engine, portal
2. **Mail.ru** - Email, portal, later investor in VK
3. **Rambler** - Portal, media
4. **VKontakte** - Social network
5. **Odnoklassniki** - Social network (older demographic)
6. **LiveJournal** - Blogging platform (highly political)

### Internet Culture
- Rapid growth of internet users
- Young, urban users most active
- Runet as alternative public sphere
- Memes, humor, piracy culture
- Less regulated than traditional media

### Digital Piracy Context
- Weak intellectual property enforcement
- Cultural acceptance of piracy
- VKontakte as major piracy platform
- Music and film industries struggling

## Political Context

### Media Environment
- Television: State-controlled or oligarch-owned
- Print: Declining, some independent outlets
- Internet: Relatively free (during book period)
- Growing use of social media for political expression

### 2011-2012 Protests
- December 2011: Mass protests after disputed Duma elections
- "Bolotnaya Square" protests
- Social media (VK, Facebook, Twitter) key for organizing
- Largest protests since 1991
- Alexei Navalny emerged as opposition figure
- VKontakte refused to limit Navalny's group

### Government and Internet
- Growing awareness of internet's political power
- Proposals for internet regulation
- Pressure on platforms to censor
- Durov's resistance notable

## St. Petersburg Context

### City Character
- Russia's "cultural capital"
- More European, liberal than Moscow
- Strong university tradition
- Tech and creative industries

### St. Petersburg State University
- Prestigious institution
- Putin's alma mater
- Strong philology and math departments
- Durov family connections (father taught there)

### Specific Locations

#### Singer House (Дом Зингера)
- Address: Nevsky Prospekt 28
- Built 1902-1904
- Original purpose: Singer sewing machine company headquarters
- Architectural style: Art Nouveau
- Distinctive feature: Glass globe on dome
- Later: Dom Knigi bookstore, VKontakte offices (6th floor)
- Location: Facing Kazan Cathedral, on main artery

#### Primorsky District (Приморский район)
- Northwestern St. Petersburg
- Near Gulf of Finland
- Soviet-era residential construction
- Durov's childhood neighborhood
- Characterized by: Panel housing, unfinished construction, marshes

#### Nevsky Prospekt
- Main avenue of St. Petersburg
- Historical, cultural center
- Major shopping and cultural institutions
- Heart of the city

## Russian Tech/Startup Culture

### Characteristics
- Strong mathematical/programming education
- Soviet legacy of technical training
- Relatively low salaries (attracting talent)
- Growing startup ecosystem
- Brain drain concerns

### Key Companies (2006-2012 era)
- **Yandex**: "Russian Google," search and services
- **Mail.ru**: Email, portal, games, later VK owner
- **Kaspersky**: Security software
- **ABBYY**: OCR and linguistics
- **VKontakte**: Social networking

### Startup Ecosystem
- Growing venture capital
- Digital Sky Technologies (DST) / Mail.ru Group
- Yuri Milner as prominent investor
- Connections to Silicon Valley

## Cultural References in the Book

### Russian Terms
- **Гопники (gopniki)**: Street thugs, working-class youth subculture
- **Ботаники (botaniki)**: Nerds, geeks
- **Нёрды (nerdy)**: Borrowed from English "nerds"
- **Олимпиада**: Academic olympiad competitions
- **Панельки**: Soviet-era panel apartment buildings

### Soviet/Russian References
- **ЦК (TsK)**: Central Committee (Soviet bureaucracy)
- **Комсомол**: Communist youth organization
- **Нарком**: People's Commissar (Soviet official)
- **Аид (Hades)**: Reference to underworld

### Tech/Internet Slang
- **Троллить (trollit')**: To troll
- **Юзер (yuzer)**: User
- **Контент (kontent)**: Content
- **Соцсеть (sotsseti)**: Social network
- **Кодер (koder)**: Coder/programmer

## Russian Business Culture

### Characteristics
- Relationship-based business
- Oligarch influence
- Government connections important
- "Крыша" (krysha) - protection/cover
- Aggressive negotiating style

### Investor Relations
- Often contentious
- Control battles common
- Short-term profit orientation vs. founder vision
- VKontakte case: Durov vs. investors

## Key Tensions

### Traditional vs. Digital
- Old media vs. internet
- Established business vs. startups
- Corporate culture vs. hacker ethos

### Russia vs. West
- Russian tech following Western models
- Localization vs. copying
- Brain drain to West
- Investment from global VCs

### Freedom vs. Control
- Internet freedom vs. state control
- Privacy vs. surveillance
- Content moderation vs. free speech
- VKontakte at center of these tensions
