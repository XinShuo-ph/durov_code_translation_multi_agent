# VKontakte (VK) - Company History and Context

## Company Overview

- **Name**: ВКонтакте (VKontakte, VK)
- **Meaning**: "In Contact" or "In Touch" in Russian
- **Founded**: October 10, 2006
- **Founders**: Pavel Durov, Nikolai Durov
- **Type**: Social networking service
- **Market**: Russian-speaking countries (Russia, Ukraine, Belarus, Kazakhstan)
- **Status in 2013**: Largest social network in Europe by users

## Launch and Early Years (2006-2008)

### Genesis
- **Inspiration**: Facebook (launched 2004)
- **Differentiation**: 
  - Russian language native
  - Better suited to Cyrillic and Russian internet culture
  - Free music and video sharing (key differentiator)
  - Faster, cleaner interface for Russian bandwidth conditions

### Initial Target Market
- University students in St. Petersburg and Moscow
- Initially invitation-only (like early Facebook)
- Word-of-mouth growth through student networks

### Technical Foundation
- **Lead Architect**: Nikolai Durov (Pavel's brother)
- **Technology Stack**: 
  - PHP-based initially
  - Custom solutions for scalability
  - Efficient code for Russian server conditions
  - Later moved to more sophisticated architecture

### First Office
- Modest space in St. Petersburg
- Small team of elite programmers
- Lean operation (famous for doing more with fewer people)

## Growth Phase (2008-2010)

### User Acquisition
- 2007: 1 million users
- 2008: 10 million users
- 2009: 25 million users
- 2010: 50+ million users
- Exponential growth without major marketing spend

### Feature Development
- **Music**: Free unlimited music streaming/download (controversial)
- **Video**: Video hosting and sharing
- **Photos**: Unlimited photo albums
- **Messages**: Private messaging
- **Groups**: Communities and public pages
- **Applications**: Platform for third-party apps/games

### Competitive Advantages
- **Content freedom**: Less restrictive than Western platforms
- **Speed**: Optimized for Russian internet infrastructure
- **Features**: More features than competitors
- **Free media**: Music/video library attracted users
- **Network effects**: Everyone's friends were already there

### Move to Nevsky Prospekt
- **Location**: Singer House (Dom Zingera), iconic St. Petersburg building
- **Address**: Nevsky Prospekt 28
- **Significance**: 
  - Prime location in city center
  - Historic building with distinctive architecture
  - Symbolic of VK's success and status

## Market Position

### Competitors

#### Odnoklassniki (OK.ru)
- **Founded**: 2006 (same year as VK)
- **Demographic**: Older users (30+)
- **Features**: Focused on finding classmates, more nostalgic
- **Market**: Significant but separate from VK's young user base

#### Facebook
- **Entry to Russia**: 2008 (Russian interface)
- **Challenge**: Never gained major traction in Russia
- **Reasons**: 
  - VK already dominant
  - Better Russian localization on VK
  - VK had free music/video
  - Network effects favored VK

#### Moi Mir (My World @ Mail.ru)
- Mail.ru's own social network
- Less successful than VK
- Eventually Mail.ru invested in VK instead

### Geographic Dominance
- **Russia**: 80%+ market share among under-35
- **Ukraine**: Extremely popular
- **Belarus**: Dominant platform
- **Kazakhstan**: Major platform
- **Other CIS**: Significant presence
- **Russian diaspora**: Global Russian speakers

## Content and Copyright Issues

### Music Sharing
- **Feature**: Free unlimited music library
- **Legal status**: Gray area in Russian law
- **Industry pressure**: Record labels demanded removal
- **User appeal**: Major reason for VK's popularity
- **Durov's stance**: Defended as user freedom
- **Impact**: Ongoing legal battles and controversies

### Video Sharing
- Movies, TV shows, user content
- Similar copyright questions
- Less regulated in Russia than West
- Major traffic driver

### Piracy Accusations
- Western media labeled VK "piracy platform"
- Russian users saw it as normal sharing
- Cultural difference in IP attitudes
- Political dimension (Russia vs West)
