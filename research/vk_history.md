# VKontakte (VK) History

## Overview
- **Full Name**: ВКонтакте (VKontakte, "In Contact")
- **Short Name**: VK (ВК)
- **Type**: Social networking service
- **Headquarters**: St. Petersburg, Russia (originally), later Moscow
- **Founded**: October 10, 2006
- **Founder**: Pavel Durov
- **Current Owner**: VK Company (formerly Mail.ru Group)

---

## Pre-History (2001-2006)

### durov.com Forum (2001-2006)
- Pavel Durov's first major online project
- Student discussion platform at St. Petersburg State University
- Gained thousands of users among students
- Taught Durov community management and viral growth
- Precursor to VKontakte's community features

### Facebook Inspiration
- Durov encountered Facebook in 2005-2006
- Saw potential for Russian-language equivalent
- Facebook not available in Russian, limited CIS reach
- Identified market opportunity

---

## Founding and Launch (2006)

### October 10, 2006
- VKontakte launched on Pavel Durov's 22nd birthday
- Initially named "Студенты.ру" (Students.ru), quickly renamed
- Focused on university students (invite-only initially)
- Core team: Pavel Durov, Nikolai Durov (technical), early collaborators

### Initial Features
- Personal profiles
- Friend connections
- Wall posts (later renamed "Microblogs")
- Photo albums
- Groups

### Early Architecture
- Built on PHP and MySQL
- Custom-built infrastructure
- Emphasis on speed and simplicity
- Nikolai Durov designed scalable backend

---

## Rapid Growth (2007-2010)

### User Milestones
| Year | Users | Key Events |
|------|-------|------------|
| 2007 | 1 million | Word-of-mouth among students |
| 2008 | 10 million | Music feature added |
| 2009 | 30 million | Video sharing added |
| 2010 | 50 million | Mobile apps launched |

### Key Features Added
- **Music (2007-2008)**: Users could upload and share music (controversial)
- **Video (2008)**: Similar to music sharing
- **Applications Platform (2008)**: Games and apps (like Facebook Platform)
- **Messages**: Private messaging system

### Competitive Landscape
- **Odnoklassniki** ("Classmates"): Main competitor, focused on finding old classmates
- **Facebook**: Limited Russian presence until later
- **LiveJournal**: Popular for blogging in Russia
- VK became dominant by focusing on youth and media sharing

---

## Business Development (2007-2012)

### Early Funding
- 2007: Minority stake sold to investors
- Key early investors: Vyacheslav Mirilashvili, Lev Leviev

### DST (Digital Sky Technologies) Investment
- 2008-2009: DST (Yuri Milner) acquired stake
- Brought professional investment capital
- Created board-level dynamics

### Mail.ru Group Involvement
- Mail.ru Group acquired stakes from various shareholders
- By 2011: Mail.ru controlled significant portion
- Tension between Durov's vision and corporate interests

### Business Model
- **Advertising**: Banner and targeted ads
- **Virtual Gifts**: Paid stickers and gifts
- **Games**: Revenue share from social games
- **Premium Features**: Various paid options

---

## Challenges (2010-2014)

### Copyright Issues
- VK's music/video sharing attracted legal challenges
- Major record labels filed lawsuits
- Russian copyright enforcement lax, but pressure mounted
- Durov initially defiant, later moderated approach

### Government Relations
- VK used for political organization (protests 2011-2012)
- Government requested user data, content removal
- Durov publicly refused many requests
- Created tension with authorities

### Corporate Conflicts
- Shareholders (Mail.ru, UCP) vs. Durov
- Disagreements over monetization, data sharing
- Durov had voting control but minority economic share
- Complex ownership structure created friction

---

## Durov's Departure (2014)

### Events Leading to Exit
- January 2014: Durov's stake sold (reportedly under pressure)
- April 2014: Durov fired as CEO
- Claimed refusal to hand over Ukrainian protester data
- Also refused to block opposition politician Navalny's page

### After Durov
- New management aligned with Mail.ru Group
- More advertiser-friendly approach
- Government relations normalized
- Platform continued growing but lost "rebel" image

---

## Post-Durov Era (2014-Present)

### Rebranding
- 2016: Renamed from "ВКонтакте" to just "VK"
- Modern logo and interface updates
- Expansion into super-app features

### Current Statistics (2024)
- 100+ million monthly active users
- Dominant in Russia, Belarus, Kazakhstan, Ukraine (pre-2022)
- Part of VK Company (renamed from Mail.ru Group)

### Ownership
- VK Company (formerly Mail.ru Group)
- Gazprom-Media, Sogaz (state-connected entities)
- Effectively under government-aligned corporate control

---

## Technical Architecture

### Original Design (2006-2014)
- Custom PHP framework
- MySQL databases with heavy optimization
- Proprietary caching solutions
- CDN for media delivery
- Nikolai Durov's algorithmic optimizations

### Key Technical Innovations
- Fast page loads (under 1 second target)
- Efficient media streaming
- Scalable messaging system
- Custom search engine

### Data Centers
- St. Petersburg (primary)
- Moscow (secondary)
- Owned infrastructure (unlike cloud-dependent competitors)

---

## Cultural Impact

### Russian Internet (RuNet)
- VK defined Russian social media culture
- Introduced concepts: "стена" (wall), "репост" (repost)
- Platform for music discovery (legally gray)
- Youth culture platform

### Political Role
- Used for 2011-2012 protest organization
- Platform for opposition voices
- Government monitoring concerns
- Changed after Durov's departure

### Memes and Culture
- VK meme culture distinct from Western
- "Вконтакте мемы" (VK memes) genre
- Public pages (паблики) influential
