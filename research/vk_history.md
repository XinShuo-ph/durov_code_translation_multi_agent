## Founding Story

### The Inspiration
- Pavel Durov, then 22, was inspired by Facebook
- Saw opportunity for Russian-language social network
- Combined Facebook concepts with local needs
- Founded on his 22nd birthday (October 10, 2006)

### Early Days
- Initial domain: vkontakte.ru
- First users: St. Petersburg State University students
- Invite-only at launch (like Facebook)
- Rapidly spread through Russian universities

## Timeline

### Growth Phase (2006-2010)

| Year | Event | Users |
|------|-------|-------|
| 2006 | Launch | ~100 |
| 2007 | 1 million users | 1M |
| 2008 | Overtakes Odnoklassniki | 20M |
| 2009 | Becomes #1 in Russia | 50M |
| 2010 | Music/video features | 100M |

### Features That Drove Growth
1. **Music Sharing**: Free music uploads (controversial)
2. **Video Hosting**: Similar to YouTube integration
3. **Groups & Communities**: Public pages for interests
4. **Messaging**: Robust private messaging
5. **Games**: Social games platform

## The Singer Building (Headquarters)

### Location
- **Address**: Nevsky Prospekt 28, St. Petersburg
- **Building**: Former Singer Sewing Machine Company HQ
- **Landmark**: Iconic Art Nouveau building with glass dome

### Significance
- Symbol of VK's presence in St. Petersburg
- Appears in Prologue of "Код Дурова"
- Strategic location on main avenue
- Historic building repurposed for tech

## Technical Infrastructure

### Key Technical Decisions
1. **Custom Backend**: Built from scratch, not off-the-shelf
2. **PHP-based**: Custom PHP framework
3. **KPHP**: Developed own PHP-to-C++ transpiler
4. **Own CDN**: Massive content delivery for media
5. **Data Centers**: Multiple facilities in Russia

### Scale Challenges
- Handling 100M+ users
- Storing millions of audio/video files
- Real-time messaging at scale
- Fighting spam and bots

## Controversies

### Copyright Issues
- Major record labels complained about music piracy
- IFPI (International Federation of Phonographic Industry) lawsuits
- Durov defended user freedom
- Eventually implemented copyright takedowns

### Government Pressure
| Year | Issue |
|------|-------|
| 2011 | Refused to block opposition groups |
| 2013 | Refused to provide Ukrainian protesters' data |
| 2014 | FSB requested activist information |
| 2014 | Durov left after refusing to comply |

### After Durov
- More cooperative with Russian authorities
- Increased content moderation
- Integrated with Mail.ru ecosystem
- Less libertarian philosophy

## VK Today (Post-Durov)

### Changes Under New Management
1. Rebranded to "VK" internationally
2. Integrated with Mail.ru services
3. Added VK Pay, VK Music, VK Video
4. Super-app strategy (like WeChat)
5. More compliance with government requests

### Current Services
- VK Social Network
- VK Messenger
- VK Music
- VK Video
- VK Pay
- VK Mini Apps

## Cultural Impact in Russia

### User Demographics
- Dominant among 16-35 age group
- Alternative to Western platforms
- Strong music/meme culture
- Political organizing (until crackdowns)
